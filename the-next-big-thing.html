What is the next big thing after big data?
Olcay Öztürk
25 February 2019
Article



We have a tendency to make things convenient and automate the processes which repeat again and again. The contemporary contents of humankind and the daily complaints of people are usually a great indicator to see what’s coming next.

I work in the big data industry and our customers are all across the industries. We offer services in data cleansing and preparation processes to machine learning product we developed during the previous months. While people enjoy the tool and ability to print the decision-tree algorithms and the pseudo-interpretation of the data, they usually ask for a complete automation if it’s possible. While we have the tools for the different stages of the big data business, we are not good at the integration of the whole process from data collection to ad-hoc analysis and even self-decision-making system which can perform the appropriate task where humans actually do after the results when IoT’s pace accelerates.

To be more specific, one of our customers in the PG(Packaged Goods) industry asked to integrate our analysis platform with an automated data collection feature which can perform to build surveys and even further reach the audiences by a couple of choices which was particularly important for the customer to make decisions about pricing optimization.

Another example was a game company which has deployed a lot of games on the mobile platforms and work with the data acquired by Google Game analytics. Their request was to automate the data cleansing process which requires more resources than any other stage of the data business. The analyst in the company said,

“We wish the data acquired with a data collection tools could be ready to analyse without a further cleansing process.”

We have a lot of tools and some of them developed by the significantly good companies which have the years of experience in the industry but somehow we are still not so close to full automation process. Like the dates and times were acquired by the devices’ setting mistakenly and it was making no sense before the data preparation process since some devices don’t have the accurate date/time settings. Can you imagine a full automation of such an error? The problem is not only how precise is the result but there is a huge investment made out of the data’s interpretation.

I don’t know what’s the next big thing which will leave a huge impact, transform businesses and decide on whom to make rich and look smart but I do know that the full automation is one of the biggest desires of us nowadays and it is partly happening now. So, the next thing, not being so huge yet impactful, is the full automation of the data businesses.

Analytics’ our passion.



Olcay Öztürk
