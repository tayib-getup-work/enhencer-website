Efficiency or Productivity
Read Article
Olcay Öztürk
09 August 2019
5 MIN READ



The last two century is shined with the fire of efficiency, while there is always rumors of productivity at some place waiting its turn on stage. Both are sometimes used interchangeably; however, even without a deeper analysis one can understand they are not same thing even a little. This fallacy comes from the everyday life of any employee since time constraints and quality of the job done is always turn everything into a competition. Therefore, in this competition one cannot differentiate one thing from another, yet alone they are undeniably close in borders of professional life. As we always stress on the paradigm shift of 21st century, we must also put our fingers on this delicate subject of professional labor life as no one should compare apples with oranges.

What Is Efficiency?

Efficiency is an easy term to define. It is so archaic that even in the times of the Neolithic Revolution, people are somewhat tried to be more efficient as they built their cities near water resources to get the most of arable land. The definition comes from the example itself. Efficiency is something about full resource use. That is, getting full out of something in terms of output. Putting as little input as possible and waiting as much output as possible. The main idea behind it is what classical economy suggest from the beginning return to scale. If there is increasing return to scale such as putting one input delivers two outputs, then you are efficient.

What Are the Benefits of Chasing Efficiency?

From the employer side, chasing efficiency is not a surprise. Gaining profits with cutting costs is the number one reason why some employers are in efficiency-mania. However, the benefits are not limited to gaining more profits, but also having more alternative to job being done. Thus, when a company follows an efficiency route for itself, the core gain would be profit maximizing with cost minimization. Working efficiently for individuals is; on the other hand, is totally about time scheduling. Individual efficiency is measured by the person’s capability to compress as many works as possible in a given time-frame.

What Is Productivity?

Productivity has also very deep roots in our society, however, it is neither old nor common as efficiency concept. The productivity is about personal limits of an individual. It is not the capability to do more work in time but capability to do more quality work. Thus, in productivity, quality suppresses quantity. Rather than doing three tasks simultaneously, productivity concept suggests doing single task but with quality. The important aspect of it comes from the person’s mental capabilities rather than their physical strength.

What Are the Benefits of Chasing Productivity?

From both employer and employee side, chasing productivity is vital. Rather than exhausting all possible resources in terms of man-power and ecology, allocating resources in order them to be most productive causes a more sustainable development. The sustainable development’s benefits are not only express themselves in the short run but also, they show continuing effects in the long run. For sustainable development, rather than just growth, physical capabilities must be left aside, and mental longings must be followed.

Which One Is Better?

The discussion of which one is better would not be simplified into this short post. However, my humble opinion on the subject is that efficiency is becoming the thing of the past. In fact, its negative pressure on workers, ecology, and economy what makes efficiency a big no-no for the 21st century. On the other hand, the productivity with its personal capacity notion and the not-so-pressure on the ecology is what it can give for the sustainable development of all agents as well as the economy itself. Thus, for the sake of the argument, the winner side would be productivity over efficiency, although it’s not about winning but sustaining.
