New Concepts in Finance Technologies
Read Article
Serkan Oğuz
26 March 2019
4 MIN READ



Seeking to improve and automate the delivery of financial technology is brand new goal of last decade. While offering original ways of customer services, firms started to look forward with broader sense. Although using technology in financial actions is as old as sector itself, with the new era of technological application, the borders of application in finance became twofold. Thus, further examination of this matter is needed in order to comprehend what would future bring to what is called FinTech nowadays.

What is Fintech

When fintech emerged in 21st century, term applied to application of technology for back-end services. However, with the increasing usage of computer, algorithms, and automation in financial operations, both back-end and front-end services made use of (or expanded) their capacity to include technology . In fact, in its initial times, fintech was used solely for back offices of banks and trading firms; however, present day applications range from money transfers, credit applications, and raising money through your smartphone to getting financial advice without consulting a human-being.

Practical Side of Fintech

The new understanding of financial practice mostly addresses to the latest generation, or at least it looks like it does. Nonetheless, recent integrations with AI, IoT, automation, cloud computing and such suggested that fintech’s area of impact is more than it is thought. In fact, the ways that expand the horizon of financial technologies reward both all customers and providers so that there would be less asymmetric information and full efficiency in the application of fintech. Although, there are hundreds of new applications and there are thousands to come, some fintech concepts are relatively new while also have more potential than others. Those new concepts for financial technologies are:

Digital Transformation

The first and foremost, as the customers become more technology oriented and infrastructure for such systems allow to implement more, financial operations are more prone to transform. The digital transformation rises with the competition between financial institutions and high expectations from customers; thus, online-only banks, automated advisory systems, and more will ornate the delivery of financial services.

Artificial Intelligence

As predicted the AI and blockchain continues to grow in applications and capacity, it will engage more critically with financial services. Teaching cognitive skills to systems (harder than it sounds), while focusing the major pillars such as deep learning and predictive analytics, would end up in a huge step towards financial sales, marketing etc. As Synechron Report for 2018 trends suggested, those concepts would highly affect financial services.

Digital-Only Banks

As mentioned above, the digital-only banks will disrupt the market for traditional banks and some other financial institutions. Although traditional banks have the historical dominance as well as a dense customer focal audience, the innovative models and new technology-oriented services appealing more and more people started to defy conventional banks. What it brings to new era is in the long run digital-only banks helps to collect more data about trends and they extend the reach of the technology.

Risk Management

Risk is almost synonymous with financial actions. Thus, it makes the risk management more important than other aspects of finance sector. Until recently and still today in some institutions, risk management is done by human operators, which is another risk factor in terms of their nature. However, thanks to improvements in the AI and automation, enterprise level and/or sector level risk analysis can be done easily through predictive analytics parameters.

Bigger Big Data

It is inevitable for big data to become even more bigger, with a proper language usage, getting more sophisticated. Financial organization relies on the legacy of the data infrastructure in order to comply with the up-to-the-minute step forward into future of fintech world. Thus, extracting data with standardized manners and adding value to this data became more and more important since data virtualization and data visualization (which also enables cloud computing in banking sector ) help us to understand how vital big data is.
