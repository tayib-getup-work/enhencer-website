How Big Can Big Data Become
Read Article
Olcay Öztürk
05 September 2019
4 MIN READ



The physical perception of the big data is discussed in our blog. In fact, big data notion is the most discussed concept in our post just as in many every other blog. This not for nothing as even the notion itself causes a paradigm shift in the data science. When data got so important, it became so because the reachability of the data sources are limited, and the conclusion derived from them were vital. As for many other breakthroughs in science, the vitality of the data becomes a thing due to military purposes. Military units using reliable data to collect information; thus, turning that information into a wisdom. As time passes, data got bigger in terms of size and the content; hence, both the application area and the methods had developed. So, what is next? What will come after the big data?

Data’s journey does not seem like it is closed to an end. However, the endings are not like normal endings, but mere shape changings. The road to big data was ambiguous at when looked from its initial times. One cannot tell what exactly happened or when exactly happened. Only thing we can say is that the term changed, the notion changed, and the definition changed lastly. Therefore, for better understanding of the information technologies’ this change in the substance, one must investigate the core of the subject. In this matter, it is fragments of data that collides into a bigger piece.

More information gathered from the sample points, would not change the definition of big data. That is, with wearable devices data collectors are collecting data of persons’ steps etc., when devices got developed, and they are started to collect our blood pressure also, this would not change the definition of big data neither makes it bigger. Big data stays the same; but, only with an extra sampling point. On the other hand, when the processing of the data changes from current concept, there will be something new, maybe bigger or not.

However, today’s information world does not suggest any dramatic changes in the paradigm of the data. It not a surprise as even big data and other concepts came with it did not fully evolve either. We cannot tell when it would evolve; however, the new paradigm taught us that it is not the time of arrival matters, but its impact does. Knowing our experience on the subject and passing it onto later generations would be perfect way to measure the impact as we even started to pass our experience to machinery so that they can evolve, too. Therefore, big data had been expanded and evolved; but we cannot differentiate it by looking it from present.

I cannot tell what will come after big data, what I can tell is that there is no need to pursue after-big-data. Big data was born when old paradigm of information was at place; however, with its coming (and tons of other factors) a paradigm shift happened, where the obsession for far future is somewhat eased. Nevertheless, the correct way to approach information technologies is to never have sharp edges. As times progresses and as needs change, the problem-solving mechanism would change as well as the problems itself. Thus, rather than trying to be the expert of the future, one must devote itself to today’s world to change the future.
