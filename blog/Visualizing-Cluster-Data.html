Visualizing Cluster Data
Read Article
Oğuz Artıran
4 April 2019
5 MIN READ



In the age of Big Data and data-driven optimality, understanding what the data says is as important as the collection of data. In fact, the further examination and analysis of any collected data are only provided with a decent sampling. Therefore, in order to get full insight from collected data, which gets larger and larger every day, the raw state of the data must be not only trimmed down but also divided accordingly. That is, the big data by itself is almost useless unless it is freed from the outliers and segmented into sub-categories according to similarities of respective sample points, which is called clustering in the new age of data science. However, things do not end here since for full comprehension, data must be presented in a way that individuals can recognize the outliers, trends, and patterns easily. Hence, this is where the visualization comes into picture. The modest examinations of these two are given below.

What is Cluster Analysis

Cluster analysis is a powerful data-mining tool for any form of the data; however, it is particularly handy when subject of analysis is huge. Therefore, segmenting the data into similar subset according to their shared features so that a proxy can be arranged is utterly helpful in big data analysis. The clustering can be done with numerous different methods however, the final product would be always more or less the same. The true power of the method comes from the distinctive characteristic of the clustering, where sampling space is reduced by creating similar subsets so that for every subset a different prediction tool can be applied. Although correct classification is hard to implement, when tackled, commonalities of each subset can be recognized instantly, which made analysis of any type unbelievably easy to implement. Although 21st century comes with a bigger than ever data, new ways of simpler analysis are being sought. Therefore, the 21st Century’s dilemma disintegrates where clustering begins.

Why to Congregate the Data

Breakthrough caused by the clustering analysis is mentioned above. In fact, the power of data collecting lies not in how big it is but in how descriptive it is. Thus, congregation of the data into similar subsets shows its power in almost all sectors where data-driven decisions are taken continuously. Identifying customers, transaction patterns and lots of other behaviors is not an easy task; however, sampling them according to their common characteristics makes pattern prediction and/or description simple as that. Therefore, after clustering one might detect some points or patterns that are not visible beforehand, which opens a door for future analytics such as predictive analysis. The predictive analysis has a huge share from clustering analysis since with a correctly distinguished clusters one can easily implement fraud detection systems, and scoring.

How to Visualize Data

After clustered data is set, the ways of comprehending it is limitless. Although, clustering is not obligatory to visualize data, for perfect analyzation of the data and make full use of visualization and clustering, one must understand how to make numbers visible and recognizable for any individual. The visualization is rather a simple step since although abovementioned clustering needs skills for statistics, econometrics, and programming, the visualization (both for making and reading) needs only a little of them. Nevertheless, one cannot overestimate their value in the data science. The presenting numerical data in a graphical or even pictorial form allows individuals to instantly recognize patterns and trends. Besides, going deep into the details of analyzation supports further examination and shows the facts behind the numbers of the data.

Why to Visualize the Data

The visualization of cluster data benefits the users in so many ways that even it can be clustered into subcategories for better understanding. However, for the time being, the sectoral benefits of the visualization will be discussed.

  • Quick responses for better operational power. The visualized data helps you to detect any pattern in the sample; thus, gives you the ability of acting faster.
  • Connecting Results and Operations. With the help of visualized data users can point out the variables that are highly related with the result in hand. Thus, any relation with the any variable and result can be detected.
  • Interacting with data for more intimate analysis. Rather than drowning in the numbers and sequences, the graphical representation of the data helps users to comprehend what is going on in the sample.
  • Storytelling. A big advantage of the visualizing cluster data is that it can provide some insight on any operation for a chosen period. In fact, with enough time-series data, a good sampling may enhance your understanding of the data, which will direct you to more efficient ways of operating.
  • Machine Learning The visualizing aspect of the machine learning is all philosophical; however, if we put it simple, the abovementioned “user” is not always a human individual. That is, clustering data and then visualizing it helps not only better understanding for humans but also things through deep learning mechanisms and IoT.
