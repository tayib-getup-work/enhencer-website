Multi-Factored Analysis to Find Single Solution
Read Article
Barış Sürücü
01 August 2019
4 MIN READ



In its mathematical meaning, the factor analysis is something about reducing and abbreviating the variables of the problem to reach the ultimate answer. Answer itself sometimes is more complicated than the initial problem. However, using a good methodology in statistics helps a lot to tackle this problem of intrinsic complication. Factoring out the unrelated variables is sometimes the best way to reach a solution. However, due to the above-mentioned intrinsic complication phenomenon which variables to be factored out is a whole new problem. Thus, sorting out all variables according to their significance, then pointing out the most viable one to use is the best way. Unfortunately, this not a easy task to do either.

Whenever I think on intrinsic complication of data science, I always think a historical phenomenon about brute force. The term brute force is widely used in computer science also to express the tackling a problem with an extensive use of same method over and over again such as using Riemann Sum derivation in order to solve an integration problem. Idea behind it is simple. Using Riemann Sum would definitely give you the answer, but it will take long time to reach than using general Integration method. Thus, as in the data science, if you have the machine power to solve the million-variable analysis, you are safe to do so. Just like in Ancient Rome where every problem from military to architecture is solved with extensive use of slaves.

However, in data science field problems are growing exponential while solving mechanisms are growing linearly. Therefore, we cannot use brute force in our example because the solution itself is as complicated as the problem. With the natural order of solving we can try to factor out the variables; but, which one? If we randomly omit a variable, we can (definitely will) neglect an important variable. Thus, problem grows more and more while we desperately try to solve it.

So, what is the solution of intrinsic complication of data science? Unfortunately, the problem is not easy to solve with a single solution. The correct thing to do is to offer a mixed solution in terms of brute force and factoring out. Although our aim is to reach a single solution, there is always indefinite amount of them in the data science. The best thing to do is to find a way to cluster variables in terms of their common features, then trying to use humble brute force to reach projections of solution. With that in mind, one can think that basic mathematical simplification is the way to go in data science intrinsic complication.

Therefore, the name multi-factored analysis comes from this idea where variables are clustered into groups in which extensive solving methods are used to reach a solution. Clustering variables in multiple groups is actually eliminating residual effect in the sets. That is, with that solution method you only try to derive the desired solution of that set and other set’s solution within itself. The term single solution is the trick since you have multiple alternative solution in multiple projections. However, these multiple alternatives are not as complicated as initial solution. Furthermore, multiple-factors are chosen such that their solutions would give a contingent alternative to the problem.

To sum up, the factored analysis is not a new topic in the field of statistics as well as the problems deriving from it. In order to reach a consistent solution, one must think all alternatives in the data compiling; however, as the big data comes around this not even possible yet alone easy. The best thing to do is to accept the nature of the big data and its exponential growth, with that in mind a mix of factoring and multiple variable clustering is more than enough to find a single solution which Enhencer would effortlessly do.
