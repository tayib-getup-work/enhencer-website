Powerhouse of Surveys: Typeform
Read Article
Serkan Oğuz
07 May 2019
4 MIN READ



New century spurred its pioneering elements in extensive use of measuring tools and analytical computing; however, no computing can be done without to-the-point data in hand. Whether you are conducting some questionnaire for a small-scale enterprise or surveying on some delicate matter such as market research, collecting correct and adequate data is utterly significant. Meaning of adequate here comes in twofold; one is that it must be large enough to conduct an error free analysis, while the other one is that it must be small enough so that neatness of the said data would not be lost.

Therefore, web-based services comes, indeed, handy for collecting those data as well as ordering them so that there would be no scattering in data, which will cause immersive workload and statistical errors. Typeform, a software as a service, assists almost anybody who wants to collect user data with a more human approach. Extensive library of options offered by the service is convenient and rich; thus, ranging from but not limited to:

  • Registration forms
  • Market research surveys
  • Branding questionnaire
  • Contact forms
  • Event registrations
  • Membership applications

Above examples and many more alternatives show that Typeform has no bound when it comes to its scope. In fact, beyond all things you can create through Typeform, they can be customizable. That is, having a more, abovementioned, human approach while preparing surveys allows you to collect the most reliable data since Typeform features a conversational type of interface where user experience is greatly affected by it (positively). Collecting feedback and/or relative information with such a tool, endorses the ability of conducting a much more consistent analysis.

Pleasing respondents with user-friendly, pleasant-looking interface helps them to focus and engage throughout the process. The conversational approach of Typeform would not exhaust the respondent with bulks of questions but it imitates a natural conversation where one question at a time is asked. Therefore, delighted and engaged respondents returns as higher completion rates for your survey.

It is not 2000’s anymore, so only depending on desktops while conducting researches is as archaic as no-computer-only-written-forms era. Hence, making the service reachable form anywhere is a great move for integration. A seamless interface which can be used cross-platforms and devices is not only boost your completion rates but also helps you to reach more people.

Best part of Typeform is that it has almost no bounds when it comes to its versality. Creating a complex IQ test is as simple as creating a survey, a registration form or a contact form. Hence, the benefits of integration of Typeform to any tool is infinitely many since its nice interface enables ease of use both for user and builder, and its conversational approach ensures one question at a time is asked so that there will be better completion rates thanks to less intimidating look of the forms, and lastly for the builder side Typeform features a real-time analytics dashboard for additional metrics such as completion rates etc.

Consequently, having the Powerhouse of Surveys (it is Typeform) in hand helps analytics to be more precise and result-oriented; thus, integration of Typeform to Enhencer is a giant step forward for better understanding of the data and the information derived from that data. Since Type I and Type II errors are most commonly arose due to lack of reliable data where amount of data and/or reliability of available data is inadequate. Nonetheless, thanks to Typeform those problems are now things of the past!
