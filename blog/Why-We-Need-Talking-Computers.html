Why We Need Talking Computers
Read Article
Oğuz Artıran
23 August 2019
4 MIN READ



The talking computers terms is like it is from a cartoon. However, the best way to comprehend what is happening in the last few decades can only be explained by computer-human analogies such as learning machines and talking computers. Although talking and learning is activities done by human beings, the paradigm of computer science let those things to be done by computers. This paradigm is fed since the first day of computer science by the future dreamers. Even the wildest dreams of future machinery consist of human-formed computers. Even they are not in human form, the dreamers attribute computers humanoid features such as feelings etc. That is why, the news on Sophia the Robot got more attention in media than Neural Networks of Artificial Intelligence.

Therefore, even though data science came to a place where computers are not limited to binary system of code, the biggest media coverage comes from the sensational “Scientists Could Take the Time Back in Quantum Computers” news. The reason behind this is same as abovementioned concepts. Believers of future want to see the most dramatic changes, immediately. The Quantum Computers are not sensational but the taking time back is a whole new level.

However, this time-travel is completely theoretical and scientific measure in which modern physics some theories became dull. That is, scientist wanted media coverage and popular public opinion (which they deserve), and they got it with a little bit theoretical trick. The learning and talking computers are the same thing in some extend. The excitement arose from the latest news by the scientists cannot find its place in the popular media until the improvement evolves into a super-science-ficitonesque.

Thus, the aim of this post is not on why we need Internet of Things, or Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence as they can prove themselves worthy without any saying -science has come so far that technology proves itself without human intervention. However, we need talking computers, learning programs, thinking and feeling artificiality in order to be the part of everyday life. When a computer learns how to re-program itself to eliminate its or others’ past mistakes, it is scientific. When a program is put into an automobile and park itself without human intervention, it is a BREAKTHROUGH. Although there isn’t any scientific difference between them, the public opinion decides which one will live. Hence, only the survivors would enhance.

Those examples and deduction can be extended into space however, our desire to know more than we have would extend beyond. The old paradigm of computer science was about retroperspective as we have mentioned in our New Paradigm of 21st Century post; however, the new paradigm is about evolving and thinking outside of the box. One must not overlook the capabilities of the human nature, but there is always beyond that. The imitation of human mind and human mindset was a good way to start. Alas, whole computer science history is full of its examples. Although we need this analogy of talking computers, we need more than talking computers itself. We need more than humans can do. If we act like we only we computers with more than one individual’s ability, we are not different than the Ancient Romans who used slaves for almost every task, however the golden days of Rome was not after civilization had millions of salves but after they started to use their brains; thus, changing the paradigm of thinking.

Long story short, what we need is to change what is happening and follow the long-flickering light of learning. Computers should talk and learn and feel, but not ONLY for self-driving cars. They must be enhanced for better alternatives and for upturns that had jumped the civilization to better places. The time between first ever plane flight (1902) and the first voyage to the space (1961) is almost 60 years. Think what did change between 1961 and 2010’s. Not only technological capabilities but also the paradigm of thinking and acting. That is why we need ML, AI, IoT and more, to change.
