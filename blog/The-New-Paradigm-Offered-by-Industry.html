The New Paradigm Offered by Industry 4.0
Read Article
Olcay Öztürk
3 March 2019
7 MIN READ



Instead of steam lines and automated assembly lines, Industry 4.0, the new era of industrial understanding, offers a far more complex interpretation of human-to-human and human-to-computer interaction. Thus, in not-so-long term, a new paradigm of sectoral behavior will emerge.

Until recently, the production and sectoral behavior was restricted to input/output systems. That is, with the what is called Third Industrial Revolution, the production was mostly controlled by AI. However, the Artificial Intelligence (AI) phenomenon -unlike its succeeding alternative- is constrained to its operator. In fact, although its implementation in the first place transformed the production paradigm ultimately, its inclination to human error and its lack of cooperative-move in terms of machines didn’t give the production boom happened when production patterns slipped from the steam engines to assembly lines (of course in relative sense).

MACHINES ARE SPEAKING WITH EACH OTHER AND TAKING DECISIONS ACCORDINGLY

The new model offers basically a tweak to the AI, in which AI’s most vulnerable part depending on a human operator; thus, being more prone to errors has become history. The ultimate benefit of New Industrial Revolution is it gives the privilege of decision-making to machines itself. Therefore, the longstanding production patterns since the implementation of division of labor and assembly lines left its position to machines which are, broadly, speaking with each other and taking decisions accordingly.

Although it has some major drawbacks such as security breaches and needing less employed personnel, they can easily be adapted to new model. In fact, security precaution improvements are evolving in line with smart factories and other places where Cyber Physical Systems (CPS) are implemented. On the other hand, the loss of high variety of employment is somewhat blurred since although some jobs will be destroyed, other opportunities will be born for labor market as economist Erik Brynjolfsson put it:

TECHNOLOGY HAS ALWAYS BEEN DESTROYING JOBS, AND IT HAS ALWAYS BEEN CREATING JOBS.”  — ERIK BRYNJOLFSSON

Internet of Things (IoT)

As mentioned above, the dialogue between machines is playing a crucial role in Industry 4.0 implementation. Using sensors to get information from other machines, rendering this information and running the operation are happening in the background where there is no human interaction. Therefore, the dialogue between machines disables the asymmetry of information; that is, machines can observe the changes or faults that a human eye cannot see. The ultimate benefits of this observation would have a huge impact on production efficiency both in terms of finding the most efficient production ways and observing a faulty aspect before it happens. In fact, this predictive mechanism offered by communication between machines is ultimately changing the industrial paradigm as we know it.

Predictive Mechanism and IoT

While benefits of predictive mechanism are clear, stressing on this will be helpful to understand why Industry 4.0 causing a paradigm shift. Across all industrial patterns, the prediction had not occupied a big role -or if so, it was prone to human error-. However, with the IoT enabling fast and reliable feedback regarding whole facility, predicting what will happen in next step became easy. In fact, since computation offers a higher work-power than a mere human, predicting the next few steps became “real”. Thus, optimizing the machinery effectiveness, which will result in a higher output level and minimized costs due to correctly anticipated maintenance times. Therefore, communication between machines, IoT, not only offers a smooth-running production; but also, offers a place where machinery predicts alternative patterns and foresee possible defects, then runs accordingly; thus, shifts the paradigm.

Industry 4.0 and Predictive Mechanism

It important to stress on IoT and Predictive Mechanism so that Industry 4.0 can be comprehended. The public awareness on the subject shows that the question is not “Is Industry 4.0 coming?”. Thus, observing the most valuable part of Industry 4.0 -predictive mechanism- became a must. Forbes writer, Bernand Marr, author of “Big Data in Practice”, states that there are some criteria to be followed if a system or a factory is considered to be run on CPS or basically Industry 4.0. Those are (Marr, 2016):

  • Interoperability meaning that there is machine-to-machine communication while human intervention is not necessary.
  • Information transparency basically means that devices, through communication, put all data into a contextual virtual world where all information is observed by other devices and even humans if necessary.
  • Technical assistance means that devices having the ability that support humans to make decisions and solve problems that are extremely hard or even unsolvable to human operators.
  • Decentralized decision making is the shining star of Industry 4.0, in its most simple sense, means that Cyber Physical Systems’ (CPS) ability of make decisions on their own.

    • All this new patterns and criteria are offering new models, since all those aforementioned decisions are made with data, reducing human error at best while being extremely fast and reliable than its preceded alternative.

    Paradigm of Industry 4.0

    One cannot put a verdict on that matter, for it is still a continuing process in which every drawback is resolved day by day while benefits are getting bigger and bigger. Thus, in terms of Industry 4.0, the correct questions to ask are always when? and how?

    Therefore, we are without hesitation sure that a new paradigm shift happened in sectoral patterns, so the predictive analysis, IoT, implementation of Cloud Computing to service sector, and machine learning have now utmost importance.

    In order to catch what is happening one must comprehend all benefits and drawbacks of changing paradigm since while early believers of this pattern will be awarded in not-so-distant future, the skeptics will be left behind.
