Filter

In order to filter specific choices from the target variable, Filter task can be used.




  1. Upon clicking the Filter task a popup window will appear providing the option to filter the target variable as the user sees fit.
  2. Blue highlighted selection means this choice(s) are selected and will be included in the analysis.
  3. Non-highlighted choices are the ones that are filtered out of the analysis.
  4. The right panel shows the choices for the variable selected for the filter task.

After filtering desired choices hit OK tick to update your analysis. In this example, we are filtering to see only the customers who are divorced and their churn proportion. Once the filtering is done the results in the middle panel will update like the picture below;




  1. Notice how the filter button is highlighted in green, this means the filter is applied on the data and the results are filtered.
  2. Blue highlighted selection means this choice(s) are selected and will be included in the analysis.
  3. In the middle section see how the results changed compared to the unfiltered result.

Interpretation:

The overall churn rate in the population is 24.95% and we are trying to observe what is the churn rate for the customers who are "Divorced". After filtering the data, it seems like the churn rate is 22.75% for the customers who are divorced which is slightly lower than the overall average.
