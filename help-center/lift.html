Lift

Lift is a powerful metric to analyze the performance of a prediction.




  1. Lift and Adjust corresponds to the same output section. They both allow users to adjust and see the captured responses and lift that the model is providing.
  2. “Counts of Target Choice” box represents the captured response analysis for the model. It is explained below here.
  3. “Proportion of Target” Choice box provides the lift analysis for the selected model.
  4. The large dots represent the proportion of data that is chosen to observe both Captured responses and the Lift. You can easily increase or decrease the cutoff threshold by clicking Plus and Minus buttons to see the changes happen.

Interpretation:
Captured Responses:
The Lift above shows that if you sort predictions from highest to lowest and get top 25% of the predictions, the average customer churn in the top 25% is 74.3% compared to overall 25% which corresponds to a lift of 2.98. In other words, the top 25% proportion of the data has a churn rate of 74.3% which is 2.98 times higher than the overall population proportion. If you change the cutoff thresholdt across the line graph you will observe different lifts for different selected proportions.
