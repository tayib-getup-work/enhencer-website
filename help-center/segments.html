Segments

Segments is a powerful tool that shows segments of the target variable and their features. Model is created segments can be accessed by simply clicking the Segments button.




  1. Right side of the analyze screen in this case shows all the segments available for the target variable.
  2. Area labeled as 2 represents the features of the selected segment.
  3. The Number in the right corner represents the sample size of the selected segment. Segment size shows how many individual records fall into that segment.
  4. The number in the left corner represents the Lift for the segment.

Segments are sorted from highest lift to the lowest lift therefore you can scroll down for many other segments and the bottom segment has the lowest lift among all the segments.


Interpretation:
What all these mumbo jumbo means in English is; We want to know which customers are more likely to become churn. The first segment contains 44 customers from the whole population with the highest lift of 4.01. Lift is explained below.

The features of these 44 customers are: Customers -

  • With online usage less than 15.1 hours per week.
  • Who have Primary Social Media Account Twitter, Instagram or Vkontakte
  • Who are from these countries: Italy, Brazil, South Korea, Canada, United States, Spain, Japan, France, United Kingdom, Turkey or China
  • With offline Usage less than 11.9 hours per week.

The features are actually the effects of the other variables form the left panel on the target variable and these are obtained by the model.

The lift 4.01 means customers in the selected segment have 4 times higher chance of becoming churn than the other customers. This means these 44 customers in this segment with the mentioned feature above has 100% possibility of becoming churn whereas in the overall population of 4000 customers on average customers have 24.95% chance of becoming churn.
